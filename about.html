“Chiropractics and The Chiropractor”

What is a Chiropractor?

A chiropractor is a licensed healthcare professional specializing in diagnosing, treating, and preventing biomechanical disorders of the neuromusculoskeletal system, which includes the bones, joints, nerves, muscles, tendons, and ligaments. In South Africa, chiropractors complete six years of rigorous study to earn their “Doctor” title.

Chiropractic care focuses on the body’s natural ability to heal itself. Through manual adjustments, chiropractors restore proper joint alignment, helping to manage pain and improve overall body function. These adjustments can enhance the performance of various systems throughout the body.

Chiropractors address a wide range of conditions and offer a holistic, drug-free approach to health. They serve as an alternative or complement to conventional medicine, aiming to reduce reliance on medications and avoid surgery whenever possible.

What Does a Chiropractor Do?

What Does a Chiropractor Do?

A chiropractor specializes in spinal manipulation, using gentle techniques to improve motion, blood flow, and overall function of the spine and other areas like shoulders, hips, and ankles. This holistic, non-invasive approach can address pain and promote a healthy lifestyle for individuals and families of all ages.

Chiropractors support the body's natural healing process through treatments like:

    • Spinal manipulation
     • Relaxation techniques
     • Heat or ice therapy
     • Stretching exercises
     • Activity modifications to reduce discomfort and aid recovery

Reasons to see a Chiropractor!

Reasons to see a Chiropractor!

The most fundamental reason is to manage or eliminate pain which occurs mostly in their lower back, neck, shoulders and head.
Signs you need to schedule your visit with your Chiropractor:
    • Limited range of motion.
     • Frequent headaches / migraines.
     • Chronic pain in joints and muscles.
     • Healing from a recent accident.
     • Whiplash.
     • Sinus congestion / feeling ill.
    • Numbness and tingling in your arms or legs.
     • You have a immobile career or lifestyle.

Our bodies are complex and respond to a variety of external stressors each day. Physical, chemical and emotional stress play a role in decreasing how our bodies ability to function optimally.

What is an Adjustment?

What is an Adjustment?

A chiropractic adjustment is the primary method chiropractors use to treat pain and dysfunction in the spine. Combining art and science, it applies gentle, targeted movements to restricted joints and tight muscles, reducing pain, inflammation, and muscle tightness while improving mobility.

Chiropractic care is suitable for all ages, including newborns, with specialized techniques designed for them. Adjustments may involve quick movements or slow, constant pressure, tailored to the patient’s needs.

The "cracking sound" often heard is simply gas released from joint fluid, similar to opening a soda can—harmless and part of the process.

The goal? To restore spinal function, remove nervous system interference, and activate the body’s natural healing abilities.

How a Chiropractic Adjustment Works!

How a Chiropractic Adjustment Works!

Chiropractic adjustments treat spinal pain by restoring motion to restricted joints and tight muscles. Safe for all ages, including newborns, they reduce pain, inflammation, and stiffness while improving mobility.

The “cracking sound” during adjustments is harmless, caused by gas release from joint fluid. The goal is to normalize spinal function, remove nervous system interference, and support the body’s natural healing.
